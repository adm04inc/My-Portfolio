About

I am an Msc student in Architectural Engineering at Chungbuk National University, South Korea, where I work as a Graduate Research Assistant in the Smart Constructions and Systems Laboratory (SCS Lab) under the supervision of Prof. Minkoo Kim. My research focuses on construction safety, particularly the assessment of workers' responses to fall-from-height hazards using immersive virtual reality (VR) environments and physiological monitoring technologies. I am interested in understanding risk perception, habituation to workplace hazards, and the application of human-centered technologies to improve safety performance in construction environments.

My work involves the design and development of VR-based experimental platforms, the collection and analysis of physiological data, and the application of machine learning techniques to evaluate workers' cognitive and emotional responses to safety-critical situations.

Prior to joining CBNU, I earned a BSc in Civil Engineering from theUniversity of Energy and Natural Resources (UENR), Ghana, where I also served as a Research and Teaching Assistant, and gained professional experience as a Site Engineer.
